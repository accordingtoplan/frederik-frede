Coffee

Brand Strategy · Identity Direction · Web · E-Commerce

"Coffee as a cultural vehicle, not the hero."

A material-driven coffee space in the LA Arts District, rooted between Berlin and Los Angeles. Brand strategy, identity direction, and a headless Shopify site — built for the crowd that already knows good things when they see them.

Concierge Coffee sits at 821 Traction Avenue in LA's Arts District — sharing an entrance with Departamento, neighboured by the city's most culturally literate crowd. Founded by Benjamin and Namy, the space is an extension of the Klein Agency aesthetic: raw, material, unhurried. The interior is their canvas. The coffee is the constant.

Working as independent creative consultant alongside the founders, the brief covers brand strategy and positioning, identity direction, and a full e-commerce website — headless Shopify with editorial frontend, built for both the LA and Berlin markets simultaneously.

01 — Brand Strategy

Between two cities. Neither explaining itself.

Concierge Coffee sits at 821 Traction Avenue in LA's Arts District — sharing an entrance with Departamento, neighbours with the city's most culturally literate crowd. The brand stretches between two cities with different frequencies: Berlin's underground credibility and neighbourhood permanence, LA's lifestyle energy and collab culture. Not trying to bridge them — just existing naturally in both.

Coffee as a cultural vehicle, not the hero.

Warm, confident, lightly playful — and never precious about it. The visual language is minimal and documentary: mood over product, hands and textures over hero shots. Packaging draws from a Bauhaus palette of muted blues, greens, and brick tones — tactile paper, embossed details, two colorways to distinguish espresso from filter. The kind of brand people recognise before they read the name.

A material-driven space to slow down.
03 — The Space

Designed by Klein Agency. 821 Traction Ave.

The physical space is the brand's first and strongest statement. Klein Agency — the husband-and-wife studio of Maša and Jon Kleinhample, based in Downtown LA — designed and fabricated the interior. Warm concrete, tactile surfaces, bespoke furniture. The kind of space that makes people slow down without being told to.

04 — Website

Headless Shopify. Full creative control.

The site is built headless — editorial HTML/CSS/JS frontend with Shopify Storefront API handling products, cart, and checkout invisibly in the background. Same workflow as the portfolio and Samsation. Shopify does the commerce; the frontend does the brand. Full bleed photography, minimal nav, sparse copy. The aesthetic of the physical space, translated to screen.
