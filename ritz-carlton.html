Event Production · Art Direction · Social Media · 2019

"A Roaring '20s gala reopens a grand hotel."

Concepted and produced end-to-end: a Roaring Twenties-inspired Golden Gala for the grand reopening of the Ritz-Carlton Berlin — PR, artist programming, interior, styling, and the rebranding of five restaurants and bars.

To celebrate the grand reopening of the Ritz-Carlton Berlin, we concepted and produced a Roaring Twenties-inspired Golden Gala from start to finish — overseeing every aspect of the event: PR, artist programming, bookings, invitations and programs, interior decoration, production and styling, right down to the layout of the champagne tower.

After several months of work, over 1,000 celebrities and VIPs attended, 500 bottles of champagne were poured, 7 artists performed and one grand hotel was reborn. We also led the rebranding and redesign of five restaurants and bars within the hotel.

01 — Identity

Art Deco reimagined for a new era.

The visual identity for the Golden Gala drew from the opulence and geometry of 1920s design — bold gold, rich typography, ornamental detail — reinterpreted through a contemporary lens. Invitations, programs, and social assets were designed as collectible objects in their own right.

02 — The Event

1,000 guests. 500 bottles. 7 artists. One grand reopening.

Every detail was considered: the champagne tower geometry, the artist set times, the lighting states through the evening. The result was an event that felt genuinely of its era — not themed, but transported.
